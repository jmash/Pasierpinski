You might recognize this as the Sierpinski triangle, which is a fractal pattern created by starting with a triangle and creating 3 triangles, each 1/4th the size of the original, on each adjacent side of the triangle, and continuing that pattern infinitely for each triangle.

However, if you hover over each square on this triangle, you'll find that the pattern is actually that of Pascal's triangle! Pascal's triangle is formed by adding together the numbers in the rows directly above. When the odd and even numbers are highlighted different colors, the pattern that emerges is exactly that of the Sierpinski triangle!
